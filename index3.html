Understanding Floaters

An in‑depth look at vitreous floaters—their causes, symptoms, and treatment options

What Are Floaters?

Floaters are tiny clumps of collagen or debris in the vitreous, casting shadows on the retina. They may be a normal part of aging or a sign of pathology.

Diagnosis & Management

Diagnosis includes comprehensive ocular exams, dilated fundus evaluation, and OCT imaging. Management options range from observation to surgical intervention.

In-Depth: What Are Floaters?

Floaters result from changes in the vitreous collagen. As the vitreous ages and liquefies, clumps form and cast shadows on the retina. Detailed clinical research explains their prevalence and associated conditions.

Diagnosis & Management Strategies

Detailed diagnostic methods include slit-lamp examination, dilated fundus exam, and OCT imaging. Treatment ranges from reassurance and monitoring to interventions such as Nd:YAG laser vitreolysis or vitrectomy.

Posterior Vitreous Detachment & Retinal Breaks

Understand the cascade from PVD to retinal breaks and lattice degeneration

What is PVD?

PVD is the separation of the vitreous from the retina. It is common with aging and can be accelerated by trauma or high myopia.

Detailed imaging and diagnostic tests, such as OCT and B-scan ultrasonography, are crucial in evaluating PVD. Early detection helps reduce the risk of retinal tears.

Retinal Breaks & Lattice Degeneration

Retinal breaks may follow PVD. Lattice degeneration further predisposes to retinal tears if not closely monitored.

The guidelines stress prompt treatment—using laser photocoagulation or cryotherapy—for symptomatic tears, while lattice degeneration requires regular monitoring and patient education.
