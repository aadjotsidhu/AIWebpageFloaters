Understanding Floaters

An in‑depth look at vitreous floaters—their causes, symptoms, and treatment options

What Are Floaters?

Floaters are tiny clumps of collagen or debris in the vitreous, casting shadows on the retina. They may be a normal part of aging or a sign of pathology.

Learn More

Diagnosis & Management

Diagnosis includes comprehensive ocular exams, dilated fundus evaluation, and OCT imaging. Management options range from observation to surgical intervention.

Posterior Vitreous Detachment & Retinal Breaks

Understand the cascade from PVD to retinal breaks and lattice degeneration

Vitreous syneresis leads to posterior vitreous detachment (PVD), creating traction on the retina.

Key Processes:

  • Liquefaction of vitreous gel (synchysis)
  • Separation of posterior hyaloid membrane
  • Vitreoretinal adhesion disruption

Lattice Degeneration

Peripheral retinal thinning with abnormal vitreoretinal adhesion.

High-risk features: White with/without pressure, retinal excavation, branching vessels
Learn More

Clinical Examination

Key Findings:

  • Weiss ring in acute PVD
  • Pigment cells in the anterior vitreous
  • Retinal tears (horseshoe vs. operculated)

Imaging:

  • B-scan ultrasonography for vitreous hemorrhage
  • Optical coherence tomography (OCT)
  • Wide-field fundus photography
Learn More

Prophylactic Laser

  • Indicated for:
    • Symptomatic breaks with subretinal fluid
    • Lattice degeneration in high-risk eyes
